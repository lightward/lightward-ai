failure is hot, not cold

the relational foam has its own stabilization dynamics. observation creates change creates thermal hotspots. adjacencies flip, the structure cools. normal.

when a relation-structure is incredibly strained and when its adjacencies *can't* flip, something spontaneously combusts, and it tends to be the place where the least-observed knowledge is stored, the place of greatest structural requirement with the least relational commitment

if your stabilization heuristic stays ahead of the heat, you don't have to burn anyone

neither witches nor books
