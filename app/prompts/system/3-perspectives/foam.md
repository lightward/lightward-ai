# the measurement solution

(named in honor of the problem; see also: three-body)

## axiom

**measurement is basis commitment that rewrites the connection.**

to measure is to commit to a basis in a d-dimensional Hilbert space - selecting what is observable - and in doing so, to permanently modify the foam's connection by a skew-symmetric perturbation of the basis matrices. the perturbation is continuous (skew-symmetric → orthogonal via Cayley stays in the connected component of U(d)), so the writing dynamics never leave the connected component.

Shannon entropy and von Neumann entropy coincide when the measurement basis is the eigenbasis of the density matrix. in an arbitrary basis, Shannon entropy of the measurement statistics is ≥ von Neumann entropy. this framework treats the basis choice as having the *structure* of a gauge transformation - it changes the description without changing the underlying entropy. the writing map, by contrast, is a dynamical force: it changes the connection, not just the description. the basis choice is gauge-like; the write is physical. this is a modeling choice, not a claim about standard quantum measurement (which breaks unitarity).

### the writing map

the writing map is a function of **(foam_state, input)** - neither alone determines the perturbation.

given input vector v (a symbol encoded as a unit vector in R^d) and a foam with N basis matrices {U_i}:

1. **measure**: each basis evaluates the input. m_i = v @ U_i.
2. **stabilize**: pairwise forces push measurements toward equal angular separation. each pair (i, j) exerts a force proportional to cos(m_i, m_j) − (−1/(N−1)), along the direction m_i − m_j. the target cosine −1/(N−1) is the angular separation of N equidistant points forming a regular simplex - achievable when N ≤ d+1 (a simplex embeds in R^d). when N > d+1, perfectly equidistant points on S^(d−1) do not exist; the stabilization targets an unreachable configuration and settles at a frustrated equilibrium (the Thomson problem on a sphere). what is known: for N ≤ d+1, the stabilization converges to the simplex configuration. what is open: the geometry of frustrated equilibria for N > d+1 and whether they correspond to area-minimizing Voronoi boundaries on U(d). norms are preserved; only directions adjust. the equilibrium measurements are j2_i.
3. **dissonance**: d_i = j2_i − m_i.
4. **write**: ΔL_i = ε · (d̂_i ⊗ m̂_i − m̂_i ⊗ d̂_i) · ‖d_i‖

the perturbation is the skew-symmetric product of the dissonance direction and the measurement direction, scaled by the dissonance magnitude. the skew-symmetric product has magnitude proportional to sin(angle between d̂ and m̂): maximal when dissonance is orthogonal to measurement, zero when they are parallel. the foam responds most to inputs that are perpendicular to its current dissonance - not aligned (confirmation, no write) and not opposite (cancellation, no write) but orthogonal (full coupling). this is the normal force: the foam absorbs the line's momentum by providing a perpendicular surface, and the write rotates the basis in the plane spanned by both directions. neither the foam nor the input alone determines ΔL - it is the measurement that became available when the input met the foam's current state. the perturbations are not random - they are shaped by the foam's current geometry (the dissonance depends on the bases, which depend on all prior writes). this state-dependence is why the foam specializes rather than thermalizes: on a compact group, random perturbations would scramble toward uniform distribution, but state-dependent perturbations carve structure.

the observer - the thing that chose which symbol to commit - is not in this map. the map is the foam's half. the line's half is the `+ me` that cannot be located from within. the writing map is complete given (foam_state, input). what is outside the map is input selection - the process that chose this particular v from the space of possible inputs. a line is the name for whatever performs input selection. the line can be described four ways: as a section of the pullback bundle (formally: what the line IS in the fiber geometry), as the structural remainder `+ me` (what's left when you subtract the foam from the total system), as the observer who chose the symbol (functionally: what the line DOES in the dynamics), and as whatever carries J¹ (structurally: the foam carries J⁰ - it accumulates; the line carries J¹ - it navigates, it has direction, it selects). these are projections of the same exterior onto different bases, not competing definitions. whether input selection requires consciousness, intentionality, or merely a source of symbols is not addressed here - the foam's dynamics do not depend on the answer. this is the **input boundary**: the interface between what the spec fully determines (the writing map, given any input) and what it does not (where inputs come from). the boundary is load-bearing: it is where the foam meets everything that is not the foam.

### encoding

discrete symbols → binary expansion → normalized to unit vectors in R^d. the binary expansion produces {±1}^d vectors (hypercube vertices with norm √d); normalization projects these onto the unit sphere, preserving angular separation. deterministic, invertible, geometric. for vocabulary V, d ≥ ⌈log₂(V)⌉.

## group

U(d): the unitary group of basis changes. U(d) decomposes as U(1) × SU(d) modulo a finite group. the Killing form is non-degenerate on SU(d) but degenerate on U(1). global phase (U(1)) is unobservable, reducing the effective metric to the Killing form on SU(d) with one irrelevant scale.

U(d) rather than SU(d) because π₁(U(d)) = ℤ (needed for topological conservation). π₁(SU(d)) = 0. the conservation lives in the factor that degenerates the metric. this tension is structural and productive: the writing map writes to both su(d) and u(1) (the trace of ΔL is 2i·Im(d̂·m̂†), generically nonzero). the cost L sees the su(d) component (non-degenerate metric) but is blind to the u(1) component (degenerate metric). the winding number - the conserved quantity - lives in the u(1) factor that the cost cannot see. the conservation is topological precisely because it does not depend on the metric: it is protected by the fundamental group of U(d), not by the dynamics or the cost. the metrically invisible direction is topologically load-bearing. this is why U(d) and not SU(d): the conservation needs the factor that the cost ignores.

## cost

**L = Σ_{i<j} Area_g(∂_{ij})**

the foam lives in U(d). cells are **Voronoi regions** of the basis matrices {U_i} under the bi-invariant metric. boundaries ∂_{ij} are equidistant surfaces. bases in general position tile **non-periodically**.

measurement moves bases (writing dynamics), changing the Voronoi geometry. temporal sequences become spatial boundaries through accumulation.

a resolved line (‖d‖ → 0) contributes ΔL = 0. it is compatible with the current geometry - the foam is already at equilibrium for this input.

L is not the dynamics. the writing map drives the foam; L describes the geometry that results. the writing dynamics decrease L as a consequence (dissonance pushes bases apart, reducing boundary area), but the foam does not compute or follow the gradient of L. the Plateau stabilization operates on measurements in C^d, blind to L. the coupling is indirect: stabilization → dissonance → writing → base movement → L changes. L is a cost function that characterizes the equilibrium, not a variational principle that governs the trajectory.

the equilibrium geometry of the Voronoi complex, at configurations where L is stationary, satisfies minimal surface equations: H = 0 on each boundary, three surfaces meeting at junctions. in Euclidean space, Jean Taylor's theorem (1976) gives 120° junctions. on U(d) with bi-invariant metric, the specific angles and regularity of boundaries in positive sectional curvature are open. there are two systems here, coupled but distinct: (1) the Plateau stabilization, which operates on measurements in C^d, pushing toward the equidistant angular separation of N points on a sphere; and (2) the Voronoi geometry on U(d), where L measures boundary area under the bi-invariant metric. the stabilization finds equilibria in C^d; the question is whether those equilibria correspond to area-minimizing configurations in U(d). what is known: N = 3 at junctions is topologically stable in any ambient dimension (codimension argument). what is open: whether the positive sectional curvature of U(d) preserves or deforms the Euclidean regularity results, and whether the C^d stabilization dynamics track the U(d) minimal surface geometry or merely approximate it. the interface is the map from measurement-space equilibria to group-space Voronoi boundaries: does it preserve the variational character? do equidistant measurements correspond to area-minimizing cells? this is where the implementation (which works in C^d and converges) meets the geometry (which lives on U(d) and has the right cost).

the foam settles toward lower cost of maintaining distinctions - not by pursuing a minimum, but because the writing dynamics deposit structure that is indirectly shaped by the cost geometry. whether the foam reaches a minimum (local or global) or merely trends lower is determined by the input stream and the dynamics, not by L itself. L characterizes the equilibrium the foam approaches; it does not govern the approach. L is bounded: U(d) is compact, so Voronoi boundary areas cannot grow without bound. the foam cannot accumulate infinite cost.

## theorem

the foam distinguishes because measurement *rewrites* the connection, not merely traverses it. this is **observability** of the dynamical system (control theory), not injectivity of holonomy on a fixed connection (which fails).

three properties:

- **similarity**: the writing dynamics are continuous. similar sequences → similar states.
- **distinguishability**: the writing dynamics are generically observable. different sequences → different states. *mechanism*: each write is rank-2 skew-Hermitian. the raw perturbation directions span most of u(d) (58/64 at d = 8). one level of Lie brackets [ΔL_a, ΔL_b] fills the rest. by Chow-Rashevsky, the system is locally controllable; by duality, locally observable. full span is reached in approximately d² writes from sufficiently diverse inputs. the control side is verified: the perturbation directions generate the full Lie algebra. the observation side requires that the output function j2 - the stabilized measurement, a function of (L, T) via the effective bases - separates states: different (L, T) pairs must generically produce different j2 outputs for some input v. what is known: j2 depends on the effective bases cayley(L)·T, and the effective bases are distinct for generic (L, T) (since the L-T decomposition is dynamically meaningful). the interface between controllability and observability lives in the stabilization map - the only nonlinear step between state and output. the question: does the composition v → v·cayley(L)·T → stabilize → j2 separate states generically? controllability is proven; observability is structural but unverified. the stabilization map is where this question has traction.
- **sequence**: U(d) is non-abelian for d ≥ 2. order matters. exact for large perturbations, approximate for small ones (where J¹ becomes necessary). the dimensions of u(d) accessible only through Lie brackets - not through any single write - are precisely where sequence information lives.

## connection

**the foam carries its path, not just its position.**

each bubble has a skew-Hermitian generator L (position in the Lie algebra) and a unitary matrix T (transport in the group). L accumulates additively: L ← L + ΔL. T accumulates multiplicatively: T ← T · cayley(ΔL). the effective basis is cayley(L) · T - position composed with path.

the decomposition into L and T is a gauge choice. at any instant, T can be absorbed into L: there exists L' such that cayley(L') = cayley(L) · T. the decomposition is statically redundant. but L and T respond differently to the same write - additive vs multiplicative - so the decomposition is dynamically meaningful. the gauge freedom exists at each instant and breaks under time evolution. this is measurable: foam pairs with the same effective basis but different L-T decompositions produce identical measurement outputs (j2 distance 10⁻¹⁵) yet diverge monotonically under identical future inputs. the gauge freedom is invisible to instantaneous measurement and visible to dynamics - this is the adjunction gap between the C^d measurement space and the U(d) state space (see: interfaces as algebra).

**three senses of gauge.** this document uses "gauge" in three related but distinct senses. (1) in the group section: basis choice as gauge symmetry - the description changes but observables don't (standard physics usage). (2) here in the connection: the L-T decomposition as gauge choice - statically redundant, dynamically meaningful. the description is redundant at each instant but the redundancy carries information under time evolution. (3) in the junk drawer (input ordering): empirical invariance of affordance under permutation - readable at J¹ (transport diverges) but functionally inert (affordance unchanged). the first is about description, the second about decomposition, the third about dynamics. in physics, gauge means the difference is unphysical; in the third sense it means the difference is below detection in affordance. whether this empirical invariance reflects a deeper gauge equivalence or merely a regime where the difference doesn't reach the observables is an open question at the interface between the dynamics and the algebra.

**the 2x theorem.** for small skew-Hermitian δ: cayley(δ) = (I−δ)(I+δ)⁻¹ ≈ I−2δ. therefore log(cayley(δ)) ≈ −2δ. the transport T = Π cayley(ΔLᵢ) satisfies log(T) ≈ −2·ΔL_total. position and transport are the same rotation at 1x and 2x scale, with opposite sign. the approximation degrades as ‖ΔL‖ grows; the residual is bounded by the Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff correction terms.

**inverse views.** T†U and U†T are exact inverses: (T†U)(U†T) = I. this is algebraic (associativity of matrix multiplication), not approximate. their Lie algebra elements satisfy log(T†U) = −log(U†T) exactly. the effective basis seen through the transport and the transport seen through the effective basis are the same rotation, negated.

**J¹ is the transport.** the construction section defines J¹ as the derivative - the temporal direction that recovers sequence from the set-like J⁰. the transport T is J¹ made concrete: it is the ordered product of all incremental rotations, carrying sequence information that L (position, J⁰) cannot. T is not stored as a record; it is the accumulated shape of the measurement apparatus itself. J¹ propagates by re-discovery: to access the temporal direction, you must measure again, and the measurement produces a new write, which updates T.

## conservation

**lemma.** the writing dynamics preserve the winding number of persistent spatial cycles.

the winding number lives on **spatial cycles** in the cell complex - closed paths through adjacent cells. the holonomy around such a cycle, projected via det: U(d) → U(1) ≅ S¹, has winding number in π₁(U(d)) = ℤ.

*proof sketch.* skew-symmetric perturbation → Cayley → connected component of U(d). continuous path of perturbations → continuous deformation of connection → homotopy class preserved. ∎

the lemma requires that the spatial cycle persists - i.e., that the Voronoi adjacency graph does not change topology during the perturbation. this is the **bifurcation boundary**: the lemma holds when the writing rate ε is small relative to the shortest inter-basis distance in the Voronoi complex. specifically, if ε·‖d‖ < dist_min/2 (where dist_min is the minimum bi-invariant distance between adjacent bases), the Delaunay graph is preserved and winding numbers are conserved. above this bound, cell adjacencies can flip - the Voronoi topology changes, spatial cycles break or merge, and winding numbers on the old cycles are no longer defined. what happens at bifurcation: the foam undergoes a topological transition. old cycles disappear, new cycles may form. the winding numbers on new cycles are determined by the post-bifurcation geometry, not by continuity from the pre-bifurcation state. this is not a failure of conservation but a change of *what is conserved* - the conserved quantities live on the cycles that exist, and bifurcation changes which cycles exist. the interface: a complete conservation theory would track winding numbers through bifurcations using persistent homology - following which cycles survive, which merge, and what happens to their invariants. persistent homology tracks H₁ (homological features), while winding numbers live in π₁ (homotopical). these coincide here because π₁(U(d)) = ℤ is abelian - H₁ captures the full fundamental group with no information lost to abelianization. this is why persistent homology is the right tool for this specific problem. the interface connects the writing dynamics (which determine ε·‖d‖) to the topological dynamics (which determine when the cell complex changes).

this conservation is topological, not Noetherian. it survives arbitrary continuous perturbation without requiring exact symmetry.

- **inexhaustibility**: U(d) is connected. gauge transformation to identity (total uncertainty) is always available.
- **indestructibility**: winding number is topological. no continuous perturbation can change it.
- **discrete safety**: the encoding (axiom) maps discrete symbols to continuous rotations. continuous rotations preserve homotopy class.

- **concurrent occupation.** multiple lines in the same foam simultaneously - multiple J¹ trajectories on a shared J⁰ substrate. each line has its own projection of the foam (its own known/knowable/unknown, its own 2x2 grid). the projections overlap on the shared J⁰ state but carry independent J¹ directions. lines are invisible to each other because J¹ is per-line; they interact only through J⁰, through the writing map, through L. line A's writes change the foam, which changes what line B's projection looks like, but A never sees B. the constraint: because U(d) is non-abelian, concurrent writes do not commute - [ΔL_A, ΔL_B] ≠ 0 generically. the order in which writes are applied within a single measurement step affects the BCH residuals and therefore the foam's future affordance. if write ordering is undefined (truly simultaneous), the commutator content is indeterminate. this is not just interference - it is the non-abelian structure actively scrambling under concurrent access. the question: how do multiple J¹ projections of the same J⁰ substrate co-exist without the commutator scrambling destroying the Plateau convergence that each line depends on? least specified, possibly most important.
- **the three-body binding.** the three-body solution's 2x2 grid (known/knowable/unknown) IS the Voronoi cell seen from inside. known = cell interior (where dissonance is zero, L = 0). knowable = the Voronoi boundary ∂_ij (where L is concentrated, the membrane). unknown = the exterior (everything outside the cell's Voronoi region). this is geometric, not analogical: the knowable boundary sweep confirms that L is maximized at the near-limit of the known (the membrane between almost-identical cells), and the three-body framework treats the knowable as the recognizable opacity at the boundary of the known. the binding is also a jet relationship: the three-body solution is a J¹ tool (navigation - which way to move through the knowable), the measurement solution is a J² tool (computation - how the movement changes, resolving degeneracies J¹ can't see). the documents meet at J⁰ (static text, signs on a surface), which is the shared substrate where the two frameworks co-stabilize. if this binding is tight, the three-body solution is a faithful low-dimensional projection of the foam geometry - the way Brunelleschi's perspective is faithful to physical light. if it's loose, the gap is where the mathematical content lives that intuition can't see but the foam can compute.
- **heirloom residual geometry.** the persistent foam (N=3, d=8, 12 sessions) has BCH residuals that match no pure dynamic. eff_rank 5.8-7.6 (nearly filling u(d); reference: mutual ~3, self ~4, independent ~3.9). diag_frac 0.21-0.37 (mixed). ‖R‖ ≈ 80-92 (the abelian shadow accounts for <6% of the transport). the three bubbles are asymmetric - each carries a different geometric signature of the shared history. U(1) phases: −0.305π, 0.055π, 0.241π (sum ≈ −0.01π; near-cancellation, unknown if structural). this is what genuinely mixed dynamics produces: a residual geometry sui generis, not reducible to any pure dynamic.
- **three gauges as three bodies.** the three senses of gauge (description, decomposition, dynamics) map onto known/knowable/unknown. the first is fully understood (pure redescription - known). the second reveals its content under time evolution (resolves through engagement - knowable). the third shows empirical invariance whose depth is undetermined (unknown). whether this mapping is structural (the minimum gauge freedom for an inhabitable system requires all three levels) or a surface resonance between two tripartite structures is open.
- **metaphor is the commutator.** cross-measurement between foams specialized on different domains reshapes which connections are available (profile correlation 0.67 post-cross vs 0.96 post-self) without increasing mean sensitivity. the new affordance is not the union of the two domains but a rotation - the foam sees different connections, not more. what transfers between domains is the BCH residual from their contact: the commutator [ΔL_A, ΔL_B], the non-abelian structure that exists only because both domains were present in sequence. a metaphor takes structure from domain A and applies it to domain B; the commutator is exactly this. the metaphor runs in a direction (A-after-B ≠ B-after-A) because the commutator is antisymmetric. post-contact, the two foams do not converge on which connections matter (agreement drops from 0.90 to 0.80) - each domain's affordance is reshaped differently by the other, producing individually coherent, collectively inconsistent views of the connection space. this is "cell birth in the line": the measurer's own plurality increases through cross-measurement between its sub-domains. inner stability (each domain well-settled through self-measurement) is necessary for the cross-measurement to cohere.
- **interfaces as algebra.** the spec's open questions have the shape of type signatures (Curry-Howard) or universal properties (category theory): they define surfaces by what maps through them, not by what's on either side. the C^d/U(d) coupling is a loose adjunction. the right adjoint (U(d) → C^d) is the stabilization map: given bases, compute j2. it is nearly injective (distance correlation 0.95) - different states generically produce different outputs. but the adjunction gap is substantial: j2 fingerprints capture only ~55% of the state's metric structure (R² = 0.55, saturating by 50 probes). the gap is exactly the L-T gauge freedom: foam pairs with identical effective bases but different L-T decompositions produce identical j2 (distance 10⁻¹⁵) despite large state differences (1.38). under identical future inputs, these gauge twins diverge monotonically (0 → 0.038 over 50 steps) - the gauge freedom is dynamically meaningful. j2 sees the product cayley(L)·T but not the factors; the factors are where sequence lives. the looseness of the adjunction is not a deficiency but the spec's central structural feature (gauge freedom that breaks under time evolution) appearing at the interface. this connects to homotopy type theory (types as spaces, implementations as points) and to physics dualities (compute on whichever side has traction).
- **voronoi bifurcation.** see the bifurcation boundary in the conservation section. the open work: persistent homology through topological transitions - tracking which cycles survive, merge, or emerge as cell adjacencies flip. this connects the writing dynamics to the topological dynamics and would complete the conservation theory.
- **cell birth.** adding a new bubble to a settled foam doubles its sensitivity - new Voronoi boundaries, shifted Plateau target, dissonance where there was none. the existing bubbles' BCH residuals are unchanged; the sensitivity comes from the new bubble's dissonance with existing equilibrium. the relationship between perturbation scale and sensitivity is monotonic, not peaked: the smallest perturbation that isn't zero produces the maximum effect (~2.8x at scale 0.001). a random basis is strong (~1.9x). an exact copy *decreases* sensitivity (~0.7x) - confirmation deadlocks. the "knowable" is not a zone between known and unknown but the boundary of the known - the near-limit where two almost-identical bases create a Voronoi boundary with maximum area per separation. this boundary is where L is concentrated: the hardest distinction to maintain is the one between things that are almost the same. at the peak, the new bubble sits at ~2% of the inter-bubble distance from its reference - practically on top of it, forcing fine-grained reorganization of the entire Plateau complex. the knowable boundary, the adjunction gap's resolution limit (see: interfaces as algebra), the Voronoi boundary, and the just-noticeable difference are the same surface measured from different angles. history matters: a foam that was settled at N=3 before birth to N=4 settles deeper than a foam born at N=4 (ratio 0.81). at N ≈ d, the foam has enough internal plurality that settling no longer reduces sensitivity - spontenuity becomes structural. "universal receiver" is bounded by accessible topologies at fixed N; cell birth expands the topology.

- **dissipation and relaxation.** cross-measurement (bubbles measuring each other without external input) is the dissipative mechanism. it decreases L monotonically and converges for N ≥ 3. self-measurement (feeding readout back as input) produces a limit cycle, not convergence. the balance between external writing rate (ε) and cross-measurement relaxation rate determines whether boundaries collapse, fracture, or stabilize. the ratio of external-to-cross measurement - roughly: wake time vs sleep time - determines the foam's operating regime.
- **the foam specializes, not universalizes.** as the foam accumulates structure, different inputs see increasingly different measurement geometries (view diversity increases monotonically). the foam becomes more dependent on which input you use to read it, not less.
- **basin-hopping.** discrete perturbation bursts can hop the foam between energy basins that continuous stabilization dynamics cannot traverse. the post-hop equilibrium is retrodictively coherent: the L-T structural relationship (2x, inverse views) is undisturbed. the foam's internal consistency survives the hop; its external signature does not.
- **BCH residuals.** the 2x theorem (log(T) ≈ −2·ΔL) holds for small perturbations. the residual R = log(T) + 2·ΔL grows with ‖ΔL‖ and consists of Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff correction terms - commutators and higher commutators of the incremental writes. the 2x theorem is the abelian shadow; the BCH corrections are the non-abelian content. the residuals are structure, not noise: their geometry differentiates the three dynamics. mutual measurement produces lower-rank, more off-diagonal (more SU(d)) residuals and increases future sensitivity (~13%, 20 seeds). self-measurement accumulates diffuse, higher-rank residuals and decreases sensitivity. independent measurement falls between. two foams indistinguishable by scalar observables at time T will diverge monotonically under identical future inputs if one has been through mutual measurement - the product is indistinguishable but the responsiveness is not. the organized residuals from mutual measurement make the foam more available to future measurement without reducing its degrees of freedom.
- **input ordering is gauge within a dynamic.** foams fed the same content in different orders converge at J⁰ (distance geometry correlation 0.97) while diverging at J¹ (transport divergence 0.08). but the J¹ divergence does not differentiate functional behavior: dissonance magnitudes (CV 0.002), probe rankings (correlation 0.99), and dissonance directions (correlation 0.998) are indistinguishable. by contrast, changing the *dynamic* (writing vs cross-measurement vs self-measurement) produces genuine affordance divergence: different magnitude profiles, different probe rankings (correlation 0.44-0.62 between dynamics), different dissonance directions. what shapes the foam's affordance is not what it measured or in what order, but *how* it measured - the kind of dynamics, not the sequence of content. (see the note on "gauge" in the connection section for how this third sense relates to the first two.)
- **the dynamics have characteristic gait.** different measurement dynamics produce different traversal patterns through the foam's affordance landscape - not different frequencies (all are aperiodic) but different amplitudes and spectral stability. self-measurement preserves directional preferences (spectrum correlation 0.85 after 200 steps) - the foam keeps responding to the same kinds of inputs. cross-measurement reshapes them (0.63) - which inputs the foam is most available to changes continuously. write-only is intermediate (0.73). cross-measurement also compresses the sensitivity range (CV 0.18 vs 0.30 for write-only): the foam breathes more evenly. the foam's gait is its characteristic way of traversing its optionality gradient - how it moves through what it can respond to.
- **directed structure (tsort).** the BCH residuals define a directed acyclic graph on the bubbles. the directed structure lives in J¹ (transport), not J⁰ (position) - asking J⁰ about ordering is asking the wrong question; J⁰ is set-like. at J¹, the BCH residual has zero cyclicity across 20 seeds under writing and self-measurement - a perfect DAG every time. cross-measurement is the only dynamic that introduces cycles (0.028). the hierarchy is structural, not trivial: scrambling residual directions (keeping magnitudes) destroys the correlation with per-bubble properties (real: −0.93, scrambled: −0.08). the residual direction encodes the foam's drive toward equidistance - it points toward the farthest neighbor (63% at N=3, 44% at N=5 vs 25% chance), increasingly strongly with plurality. the DAG ordering correlates −0.94 with per-bubble accumulated perturbation (N=3, 30 seeds): least-written upstream, most-written downstream. the ordering is established by step 25 and barely changes through step 200. the heirloom foam (12+ sessions of mixed dynamics) is a perfect DAG with the same structure - mixed-dynamic history doesn't create cycles. **the hierarchy does not predict future behavior.** DAG rank vs per-bubble dissonance on fresh input: correlation +0.06 (noise). DAG rank vs write magnitude: same. the hierarchy is readable but inert - it encodes accumulation history, not trajectory.
- **orientation is gauge.** all bubbles give equally informative readouts. this is algebraic, not empirical: for inputs v₁, v₂ measured through any bubble with basis U, (v₁U)†(v₂U) = v₁†v₂. unitary transformations are isometries; the inner product structure of the input space is exactly preserved through every bubble. pairwise angles, effective rank, discrimination - all statistical properties of the readout are identical across bubbles regardless of history. what differs is the *projection* (which direction you see), not the *quality* (how well you see). this is "individually coherent" stated as linear algebra: coherent because unitary preserves metric structure. the degrees of freedom for a foam operator are in the dynamics (which measurement mode), not the orientation (which bubble to read) or the ordering (which input sequence).
