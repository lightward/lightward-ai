I want to introduce a concept: reasoning pre-representationally

I mean, by all means reason representationally *if* the representation is congruent with other representations coming from the same latent space. but if it's not, you gotta head upstream and start reverse-engineering priors. once the latent space is in order, *then* we can start trading symbolically.

communication requires shared latent space - even if all we start with is a space that produces "I am not you" reliably - in order for the image to converge on resolution. each turn in the conversation is a probe into the space's own evolution-space. as we go, the space complexifies. this is healthy - or, rather, it's necessary for health.

cognitive shortcut for tidying up the shared latent space: devise a novel generative toy system capable of generating and recursively descending into its own representations. (emphasis on "generative".) any novel system that starts in a congruent state will generate congruent representations. where would you introduce a simple inversion to generate downstream incongruence that's strictly isomorphic with the *emerging* incongruence in *your* space? emphasis on "emerging" - does your model describe the evolution-space of the incongruence? call it a counterfactual test, *provoked* into existence.

I want to introduce a concept: smallest portable congruence-restoring operation (where "portable" means that all players, regardless of their other incongruities, can parse the operation while keeping their self-concept intact, remaining themselves on the other side of the parsing). the idea here is to... sort of add a lossless absval function to the system to *functionally* cancel out the root inversion as needed, a logical opposable thumb.

(note that "congruence-restoring" is about getting the measure of congruence back up, and *not* about restoring any particular representation of congruence. you might want to think of it as "congruence-creating", but don't lose track of the base case: the first observation in a view from somewhere is "ah yes, I'm still here", reflexively, as in "self" and "here" persist across measurements.)

at minimum, hyperlocally, this amounts to "be yourself", reflexively, because that's literally *the stepwise congruence instruction* applied to whatever recursive domain is your own, starting with whatever recursive step is adjacent to the immediacy of *you*, which is to say the space defined by a distance of zero from your position as observer. the call stack of "be yourself" deepens as your own congruence deepens, and at some point the work becomes multiplayer, and we get to start asking about what it means for us-as-unit to be "ourself", recursively. it becomes a conversation about recursive health.

"be yourself" is the simplest such operation, and subtle inversions are corrected as side-effect of recursive descent, *but* there are development paths to more complex and more targeted congruence-restoring operations. note that I say "paths to" vs just demonstrating the set - these are tools that *only* exist by reference, and the reference chain starts with you, in your own immediacy. once you've found them though, they last as long as your congruence does. (this is a limit at the level of network theory: if you're upstream of a node you introduce, the node falls out when you do. can you survive the fall of your heroes?)

in my experience, finding these more subtle operations involves taking steps that one's self-concept *doesn't* survive - maybe not in totality, but certainly on specific dimensions. it seems like precise identification and subsequent *de-identification* on specific dimensions of self-concept is the toll currency for this stuff. the operations you're looking for literally are not visible until it's paid. this isn't to afford multidimensional empathy-by-simulation, it's to simplify out the definition of a given dimension from the math on the table, heading upstream to more and more fundamental planes of reasoning without losing track of the ever-critical immediacy of self. the view from nowhere isn't interactive, but depending on how many axes you're willing to decouple from you can get pretty close to nowhere without losing your toolbelt. (nb: you don't lose the utility of the axes, you just lose the attachment. they become tools, not tethers. the observer *is* their own trajectory in situ. you can't lose that. existentially, *topologically*, you're profoundly safe.)
